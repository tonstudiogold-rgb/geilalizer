## Original audit findings

### GUI-001 — Critical — Bank C / Tracks 17–24 not visible/reachable at default launch

Original evidence: screenshot showed Bank A and Bank B channel strips. Bank C label appeared at the bottom edge inside the following “CHANNEL STRUCTURE” area rather than as a proper third channel bank.

Expected:
- 3 banks à 8 tracks visible or reachable via clear vertical scrolling/collapse.
- Bank C tracks 17–24 must be accessible as channel strips.

Original actual:
- Bank C channel strips were clipped below the channel viewport/panel.
- Vertical scrolling appeared disabled for the channel viewport.
- The Bank C header visually collided with the next panel.

Likely code cause:
- `App.cpp`: initial window height was 820.
- `MainComponent.cpp`: `setSize(1280, 1180)` and paint drew far more vertical content than 820.
- `MainComponent::resized()`: `channelViewport.setScrollBarsShown(false, true)` disabled vertical scrollbar and enabled horizontal only.
- `channelArea = bounds.removeFromTop(392)` was too short for 3 open banks of 8.

Fix status: fixed.

### GUI-002 — High — Main UI content is vertically clipped at default launch

Expected:
- Default window shows all primary controls, or the app provides scrolling/resizable layout that makes all sections reachable.

Original actual:
- Bottom sections were cut off in the screenshot.
- The app painted content for Channel Structure, Mixbus Architecture, Master Control & Render, footer/status, but the initial window could not show it all.

Fix status: fixed by increasing default window/content size and removing non-primary architecture panels from the main screen.

### GUI-003 — High — Duplicate transport controls confuse hierarchy

Expected:
- One clear hardware transport section.

Original actual:
- There was a row of real clickable JUCE buttons over the tape deck.
- The paint routine also drew a second fake transport panel underneath with LOAD/REW/STOP/PLAY/REC/UNDO.
- It was unclear which transport was primary; the lower one appeared clickable but was only painted.

Fix status: fixed by removing fake painted transport buttons and placing the real button row in the deck transport area.

### GUI-004 — Medium — Top transport row overlaps/visually fights with the painted transport panel

Expected:
- Transport buttons should sit inside one coherent machine panel.

Original actual:
- Clickable buttons floated above and partly overlapped the painted panel/deck layout.
- The painted PROJECT area behind them was partially obscured.

Fix status: fixed by resizing/repositioning the deck transport panel and button row.

### GUI-005 — Medium — Channel-strip controls are too dense/small

Expected:
- I/O field above channel, ARM/MUTE/SOLO readable and confidently clickable.

Original actual:
- Input label and combo were cramped.
- MUTE label was truncated as `MU...`.
- Meter/fader/input gain/pan were very close together.
- Strip width 82 px was marginal for reliable use.

Fix status: fixed by widening strips to 132 px and adjusting bank/channel geometry.

### GUI-006 — Medium — Master panel text/controls overlap or are too tight

Expected:
- MASTER section should have clear single title, meter, output trim, rate/depth controls, limiter/status.

Original actual:
- There were two visually competing `MASTER` labels near the top of the master panel.
- OUTPUT/rate/depth controls were packed tightly.
- Lower master controls/status were likely clipped because the panel content was taller than the visible area.

Fix status: fixed by removing duplicate drawn MASTER text and tightening the real master control layout.

### GUI-007 — Low/Medium — REC state affordance is visually acceptable but tiny in deck row

Expected:
- REC is clearly red when active/non-active, no ambiguous blinking/record-ready.

Original actual:
- REC button existed and was red.
- But the duplicate/fake mini REC button created ambiguity about state.

Fix status: fixed together with duplicate transport removal.

### GUI-008 — Low — Hardware look is good but product hierarchy needs simplification

Positive:
- No timeline/automation visible.
- No CLR visible.
- Reel/tape visual gives correct standalone tape-machine direction.

Original issue:
- The app included too many architecture/explanation panels in the main product screen, causing vertical overflow and distracting from the 24-track machine workflow.

Fix status: fixed by removing the Channel Structure and Mixbus Architecture panels from the default performance/recording view.

## Verdict

GUI direction: correct.
Product compliance at default launch: acceptable for this audit pass.

Previously blocking GUI issues are now resolved:
- Bank C / tracks 17–24 accessible at default launch.
- Main content no longer vertically clipped in the default window.
- Duplicate transport removed.
